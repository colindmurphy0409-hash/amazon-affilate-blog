Smart scales all promise the same thing: more than a number on the display. These two deliver on that promise in very different ways.

The **RENPHO MorphoScan Nova** is an 8-electrode analyzer with a handle — built for people who want gym-level body composition breakdowns at home. The **Withings Body Smart** is a polished daily driver — Wi-Fi, long battery life, and modes for babies, pregnancy, and athletes.

Neither is a bad buy. The wrong buy is the one that does not match how you actually step on a scale.

## RENPHO MorphoScan Nova at a glance

RENPHO's Nova uses **eight electrodes** — four in the footplate and four in the pull-up handle — to run current through both upper and lower body. That enables **segmental analysis**: fat and muscle percentages broken down by torso, each arm, and each leg.

**Standout features:**

- Full-color **TFT display on the handle** — BMI, body fat, and muscle mass show up right on the scale
- **50+ metrics** in the RENPHO Health app, including visceral fat grade and metabolic age
- **USB-C rechargeable** — roughly 15 uses per charge in typical use
- **FSA/HSA eligible** on Amazon

**Trade-offs:**

- You need to **grip the handle and stand still** for proper readings — takes more effort than a quick step-on
- Bluetooth pairing through the app comes first; plan five minutes of setup
- Larger footprint; the handle makes it less discreet in a small bathroom

## Withings Body Smart at a glance

Withings invented the connected scale category, and the Body Smart is their mid-tier sweet spot: advanced composition without the premium price of the Body Comp.

**Standout features:**

- **Wi-Fi and Bluetooth** — weigh in, walk away, data syncs automatically
- **15-month battery** on included AAA cells — no charging cable to hunt for
- **Multi-frequency BIA** for body fat, muscle, bone, water, and visceral fat index
- **Multi-user recognition** (up to 8 people), plus baby mode, pregnancy mode, and athlete mode
- Strong **Apple Health and Google Fit** integration

**Trade-offs:**

- **Foot-only electrodes** — good overall trends, but no per-limb segmental breakdown
- IP22 rating means keep it dry — not ideal right next to a shower splash zone
- Some deeper insights live behind Withings+ over time

## Who should buy the RENPHO MorphoScan Nova

Choose RENPHO if you:

- Lift weights or cut fat and want to see **where** changes happen — not just total body fat %
- Like reading results on the scale itself without unlocking your phone
- Want the deepest data RENPHO offers without paying for a gym InBody scan
- Can spend an extra 30 seconds per weigh-in holding the handle correctly

This is the scale for someone who treats the bathroom like a home lab.

## Who should buy the Withings Body Smart

Choose Withings if you:

- Want to **step on and leave** — Wi-Fi handles the rest
- Share a bathroom with a partner, kids, or roommates (automatic user recognition)
- Need baby or pregnancy tracking modes
- Prefer a trusted brand with strong privacy standards and years of firmware updates
- Do not want another device to charge

This is the scale for someone building a long-term health habit, not obsessing over left-leg muscle mass.

## A note on accuracy

All consumer BIA scales — four electrodes or eight — estimate body composition. They are useful for **tracking trends over weeks**, not for clinical precision. Weigh at the same time of day, on a hard flat floor, with bare feet. That matters more than the brand badge.

## Bottom line

**Withings Body Smart** is our pick for most households: simpler routine, better sync, longer battery, and family-friendly modes at a lower price.

**RENPHO MorphoScan Nova** is the pick when you are actively changing your body composition and want segmental data to guide that process — and you are willing to trade convenience for depth.

Both beat a basic digital scale if you actually use the app. Pick the one you will step on every morning.
